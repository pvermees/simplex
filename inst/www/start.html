Processing SIMS data with simplex is simple:
  1. setup: load one of the built-in datasets, or choose one (for SHRIMP) or multiple (for Cameca) datafiles of your own.
  2. drift: click on the eponymous button and apply a drift correction to single collector data. This correction is not necessary for multi-collector data. Therefore, the drift option is hidden for the oxygen and sulphur isotope methods.
  3. logratios: click on the eponymous button and fit the drift-corrected data with logistic normal model that is linear in time. This calculation might take up to a minute.
  4. calibration: choose a reference material and specify the corresponding aliquots in your dataset by entering the prefix in a text box. Then click Calibrate.
  5. samples: apply the calibration to the samples by clicking Calibrate. If desired, you can specify a subset of the aliquots by entering their prefix in a text box.
  6. finish: plot, tabulate, or export the data to IsoplotR.
