Applies the calibration curve of tab #4 to (a selection of) samples.
Calibrate:
Calibrate the samples and plot the results on a version of the calibration plot that is augmented with age estimates (for geochronological data) and δ-values (for stable isotope data).
Label aliquots:
Ticking the box uses the row numbers of the table of aliquots to label the calibration plot.
Tabulate:
Produces a table of calibrated isotope ratios with standard errors and covariances. Aliquots whose backgrounds exceed some signals (typically 204Pb) are marked with an asterisk (*).
log:
Ticking this box reports the calibrated data as logratios. Unticking it exponentiates those values.
cov:
Ticking this box returns the output as a vector of logratios and its associated covariance matrix.
exterr:
Ticking this box adds the uncertainty of the calibration curve to the uncertainty budget, resulting in inter-sample error correlations of the Pb/U- and/or Pb/Th-ratios.
Select samples by prefix:
Specify the names of the aliquots that are to be calibrated by entering their first few characters.
Aliquots to analyse:
Alternatively (or additionally) to the prefix box, individual aliquots can also be added or removed by entering 'yes' or 'no' in the appropriate cells of the table.
